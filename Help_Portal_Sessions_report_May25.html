Key Insights
  • Google Organic is the dominant driver — lost 8M sessions YoY (−25.9%) Google organic dropped from 31M to 23M sessions (Mar 2024–Feb 2025 vs Mar 2025–Feb 2026). This single channel accounts for the overwhelming majority of overall decline. Yahoo organic (−46%), Reddit (−52%), and other organic sources compound the signal.
  • Knowledge Articles are the core content losing traffic (−21.8%, −3.5M sessions) The /articleView URL lost 4.4M sessions (−9.1%) and KBKnowledgeArticle content type dropped 21.8%. Users are finding article-level answers without ever landing on help.salesforce.com — consistent with AI answer extraction from Google.
  • APAC and emerging markets hit disproportionately hard China −44%, South Korea −32%, Vietnam −31%, Taiwan −36%, Philippines −27%. These markets show 2–4× more erosion than the US (−9%), suggesting stronger AI/LLM adoption or different search engine algorithm changes in those regions.
  • Tableau and MuleSoft referral traffic collapsed (−64% and −73%) kb.tableau.com dropped from 602K to 215K sessions. help.mulesoft.com from 277K to 75K. These cross-property referral flows have largely broken — possibly due to those properties shifting their own help ecosystems.
  • Case submission traffic declining (−21%), search traffic −29% /search-result sessions fell 273K and /case-submission fell 266K. The case submission drop is largely attributable to users now creating cases through Agentforce, bypassing the traditional help.salesforce.com case submission flow entirely.
  • ChatGPT referral grew +344% — AI is sending some users back chatgpt.com referral sessions grew from 78K to 345K (+267K). This is a small but fast-growing channel, suggesting that while AI tools are cannibalizing organic search traffic, they also occasionally surface help.salesforce.com links directly.
  • Direct traffic up +29% and case management pages growing Direct sessions grew by 5.4M, /s/cases +35%, /s/case-view +21%, /s/login +11%. Logged-in and support-workflow usage is healthy and growing — the decline is entirely in discovery/self-service via search.
  • Einstein / AI product pages surging: +148% on /products/einstein /s/products/einstein up 147%, Data Cloud +37%, Platform +47%, Proactive Monitoring +44%. Newer AI-era products are driving incremental traffic even as legacy content declines.
